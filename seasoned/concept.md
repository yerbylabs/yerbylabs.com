# Seasoned

> *Every ingredient has a story. Every cook has a journey.*

---

## What It Is

**Seasoned** is a real-world culinary exploration app that turns farmers' markets, farm stands, and local grocers into an adventure. Instead of starting with a recipe and handing you a shopping list, Seasoned starts with the world — what is growing near you right now, what is at the market this week, what is worth discovering this season — and turns the act of finding and cooking with those ingredients into a game.

It is the Yerby Labs philosophy applied to food: software that pulls you toward the world, not away from it.

---

## The Problem It Solves

The food app landscape is overwhelmingly transactional. Recipe managers help you organize what you already know. Meal planners optimize for efficiency. Grocery apps reduce the market to a logistics problem. None of them ask the most interesting question: *what is actually worth eating right now, and what could you do with it?*

Seasoned fills that gap. It is not a utility — it is an exploration app. It transforms the mundane task of grocery shopping into a treasure hunt and the act of cooking into a creative quest.

---

## The Core Loop

The experience is built around three repeating actions that move the player from screen to world to kitchen.

1. **The Forage (Discovery):** Each week, the app presents a "Bounty Board" populated with seasonal ingredients based on the player's geographic location and the current micro-season. A player in the Pacific Northwest in October might see Chanterelle mushrooms, Honeycrisp apples, and Delicata squash. A player in the Gulf South in April might see Vidalia onions, Satsuma citrus, and fresh Gulf shrimp.

2. **The Harvest (Logging):** The player goes out into the world — to a farmers' market, a farm stand, a specialty grocer — to find these ingredients. When they do, they log the item in their **Pantry Journal**. This is not just a checklist. Logging an ingredient unlocks rich, beautifully written lore: its history, cultural significance, flavor profile, and traditional uses across different cuisines.

3. **The Feast (Creation):** Once an ingredient is logged, Seasoned does *not* immediately hand over a recipe. Instead, it offers **Flavor Pairings** (e.g., Delicata squash pairs well with brown butter, sage, and pecorino) and **Technique Challenges** (e.g., "Try roasting this at high heat until caramelized"). The player is encouraged to experiment. When they cook a dish, they log the result with a photo and notes, earning XP and advancing their culinary skill trees.

## Key Mechanics

### Culinary Skill Trees
Players do not just level up generically. They advance specific culinary disciplines. Cooking with five different alliums levels up the "Aromatics" branch. Successfully fermenting an ingredient advances the "Preservationist" tree. Mastering three different preparations of a single protein unlocks the "Deep Cut" badge for that ingredient. Over time, players build a rich, personalized culinary identity.

### The Pantry Journal
The Pantry Journal is the heart of the app — a living, personal record of every ingredient discovered, the season it was found, and the dishes created from it. Over months and years, it becomes a deeply personal interactive cookbook that reflects the player's geography, curiosity, and growth as a cook. No two Pantry Journals will look alike.

### Community Market Pins
Players can drop location pins on a shared map to alert their local community when a hard-to-find or especially good ingredient is available at a specific market or farm stand. First ramps of the spring at the Saturday market? Drop a pin. The heirloom tomato vendor is back? Drop a pin. This builds a hyper-local network of food-curious people helping each other find the good stuff.

### Seasonal Events
The app changes with the natural calendar. Limited-time events — "The Great Citrus Harvest," "Stone Fruit Season," "The Allium Festival" — offer special bounties, unique badges, and community challenges that drive urgency and bring players back at key moments in the culinary year.

## Why This Is a Yerby Labs App

Seasoned executes the Yerby Labs mission precisely. It pulls people away from their screens and into their local communities — the farmers' market, the farm stand, the specialty grocer. It encourages a deeper relationship with the natural world through the lens of food and seasonality. It rewards curiosity over convenience, which is the exact opposite of every other food app on the market.

It also extends the proven IronBeak formula — real-world discovery, field journal, RPG progression, community — into a domain that touches everyone's daily life. Where IronBeak asks *what birds are around you?*, Seasoned asks *what is growing around you?* The instinct is the same. The world is the game.
